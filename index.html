üåç About the France Telugu Students Group

The **France Telugu Students** community was founded to support students, primarily from India's Telugu-speaking regions, who are navigating the journey of studying in France. We understand the unique challenges of relocating across continents, from application stress to settling into a new culture.

Our primary **purpose** is to provide accurate, timely information, share personal experiences, and foster a supportive network. We aim to ease the transition for new students and help existing students thrive academically and socially.

This community is an essential resource for both **current students** in France and **abroad aspirants** planning their move. Whether you need advice on visa interviews, finding accommodation, or simply want to connect with others who speak your language, you'll find your home here.

**We believe in peer-to-peer support.** Our group is a platform for students, run by students, ensuring the advice you get is practical and grounded in real-life French student experiences.

üìú Group Guidelines: Our Community Code of Conduct

  1. **Be Respectful:** Treat all members with courtesy. Abusive language, trolling, or personal attacks are strictly prohibited.
  2. **Stay Relevant:** Keep discussions focused on studying in France, student life, visas, or related topics. Avoid non-relevant forwarding, memes, or unsolicited advertisements.
  3. **Protect Privacy:** Do not share personal information of other members without their explicit consent.
  4. **No Illegal Activities:** Do not discuss or promote any illegal activities, including unauthorized immigration practices or fraudulent document preparation.
  5. **Avoid Unverified Information:** Share only verified, factual information, especially regarding official processes (visa, applications). State clearly when you are sharing personal opinion or experience.
  6. **Respect Admins:** Follow directions and decisions made by the group administrators. They are here to maintain order and helpfulness.

üß≠ Essential Guide for Indian Students Coming to France

1. Foundational Steps

  • **Passport:** Ensure your passport is valid and won't expire during your stay.
  • **Choosing Course:** Research and finalize your university and program of study.
  • **Exams:** Complete necessary English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL) and any required aptitude tests.

2. Applications & Admissions

  • **Documents:** Prepare academic transcripts, LORs, SOPs, and CVs.
  • **Applications:** Submit applications to universities (often via Campus France).
  • **Admission Letter:** Secure your official Letter of Acceptance (LOA).

3. Visa & Finances

  • **Visa Process:** Complete Campus France interview and submit your VFS Global application.
  • **Financial Prep:** Arrange blocked account (attestation), proof of funds, and tuition fee payments.
  • **Insurance:** Secure mandatory student health insurance (Campus France/private).

4. Travel & Arrival

  • **Flight & Packing:** Book your one-way ticket and pack essentials (clothes, adapter, limited food).
  • **Accommodation:** Finalize student housing or temporary stay arrangements before flying.
  • **First Week:** Validate your visa (online or in prefecture) and register at your university.

5. Settling In & Resources

  • **Jobs (Student):** Learn regulations for part-time work (964 hours/year).
  • **Essential Apps:** Download RATP, VTC (Uber/Bolt), banking, and translation apps.
  • **Emergency Contacts:** Keep police (17), ambulance (15), and fire (18) numbers handy.

6. Final Checklist

Before Departure: Original documents, flight ticket, proof of accommodation, minimum cash.

First Month: Open a French bank account (e.g., LCL, BNP), apply for CAF (housing aid), and get a SIM card.
